Basic Statistics

Note:

  1. Total Pax indicates the total number of passengers traveling from this origin from two databases: DB1B and T100. Differences could exist between these two sources because they are collected in different ways.
  2. DB1B is based on a 10% sample of itineraries. For each origin, it only contains the trips that start from the origin and head for a final destination within U.S. territory. That means intermediate stop would not be counted as a departure.
  3. Some small regional carriers are not required to report in DB1B. So Total Pax in DB1B shows the estimated departures from the origin airports.
  4. T100 data presents the domestic enplanements at each airport. Enplanement is the number of passengers boarding an aircraft at an airport(both starting from that airport or connecting at that airport). However, enplanement does not include 'through' passengers (those who stop at an airport and continue their trips on a flight sharing the same flight number with their previous flight).
  5. Total Pax in T100 shows the number of enplanements from the origin airports.
  6. The domestic legs of an international trip would appear in T100 data.
  7. Direct(%) indicates the proportion of passengers that reach destinations directly from this origin in DB1B.
