The original version of Sky Explorer focuses on T100 Air Carrier Statistics, provided by Bureau of Transportation Statistics(BTS) in United States. T100 is an exellent data source with plenty valuable information. However, T100 data only provides the aviation data for United States. To make provide better data to the user, we collect and present the data from the aviation authorities outside United States in this new version.

Please note that not all the data source provide very detailed information as T100. In general, US BTS T100 offers the most detail information, including flight numbers, passengers, freight and airline, of each route which at least has one endpoint in United States. Some data sources only give a brief summary of the total flow of both directions in each route. Therefore, the system generates various UI for different data sources.

US BTS T100 and T100 FF

Form 41, Schedules T100, which is an excellent database provided by Bureau of Transportation Statistics, contains comprehensive monthly aggregated records of detail information of the flights start or end within the US territory (including Puerto Rico and Guam) for both passenger and freight. Each record in the database is an aggregated entry for all the flights with the same origin, destination, carrier, aircraft type and month. T100 Data has a column indentifying whether a record is charter flight, which is used by our program to filter the data. Only the airlines operating schedule flights will be selected into our database.

Data Bank 28IS(FF) provided by BTS is also used as supplementary data source. It contains the international segments for US air carriers. However, it has a 3-year restriction. The system only covers this data from 2003 to 2009.

Korea Airport Corporation Data

Korea Airports Corporation is the an organization specializing in airport management. KAC manages and operates total of 14 airports in Korea including Gimpo, Gimhae, Jeju, Daegu, Muan, Cheongju, and Yangyang international airport. It provides brief monthly air statistics of international and domestic routes.

Note: The largest airport in South Korea, Incheon International Airport (ICN), is not managed by KAC. Therefore KAC data doesn't include the statistics for ICN.

Taiwan CAA Data

The Civil Aeronautics Administration, Ministry of Transportation and Communication is a government agency of the Ministry of Transportation and Communications of the Republic of China which is responsible for the regulation of all civil aviation activities in Taiwan. It releases monthly and annual report with passenger aviation statistics.

Wikipedia Airport Information Page

Wikipedia provides detailed introduction of most civil airports all over the world. In these pages, a table shows available destinations with operating airlines. Although this data is not as accurate as the official time table released by airlines, it is still a trustable resource of available connections of an airport.

All the airport data is obtained from Wikipedia English version.
